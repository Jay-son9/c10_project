About the project

The Chevrolet 1967-1972 pick-ups, Blazers, and Suburbans have held a special place in my heart since my youth. Over the years, I've been fortunate to own two Blazers, two C/10 trucks, and a 1971 GMC, while my father had a 1969 Suburban. Growing up alongside my skilled father in the garage, I developed practical skills in painting, welding, mechanics and basic bodywork, enabling me to maintain and repair my own vehicles. After searching for approximately five years, I finally came across a promising truck. While the paintwork was impressive, some areas required attention. Nevertheless, my main objective was to minimize the amount of necessary bodywork, even though I acknowledged that addressing it would become inevitable. The truck came with a problematic 305 engine and TH350 transmission, which I disliked but couldn't replace immediately due to budget constraints. Despite the engine's shortcomings, I focused on improving other aspects of the truck. The electrical system underwent a major overhaul, including the installation of a new instrument bezel, instrument cluster lens, and restoration of existing gauges. Additionally, I added sound deadening, custom removable diamond plate elements, and enhanced the dash lighting with LEDs. Numerous other upgrades and improvements were made, as detailed in the parts inventory. To enhance the truck's appearance and performance, I lowered the front by 2 1/2 inches and the rear by 4 inches, and installed new rims and tires. While I encountered challenges in achieving consistent performance with the 305 engine, I remained determined to drive beyond the local area.

This project has been a remarkable endeavor thus far. Although this truck is not intended to be a show truck and is not without imperfections, I have gained valuable insights through the mistakes I made, such as ordering incorrect parts and attempting to work on multiple aspects simultaneously. Thorough research has been necessary, especially considering that the 305 engine was not originally installed in the truck. Based on the block identification (ESN), it originated from a 1985 Monte Carlo. The majority of the components were sourced from reputable online racing stores and specialized websites catering to custom cars, which I have provided links to below. It is worth noting that this vehicle is 53 years old, and accordingly, it demands extra attention and care. I eagerly anticipate the installation of the new engine. Furthermore, there are additional modifications and accomplishments that I may have overlooked, which I will document and include as I progress. I possess all the receipts for the new parts, as well as the necessary hardware, paint, and powder coating supplies. Until then, this remains an ongoing project.
